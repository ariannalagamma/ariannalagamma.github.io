003 /

Inuit Indigenous communities are geographically located in the northern regions of Canada. Nunavet is the province most densely populated with Inuit individuals, followed by Quebec, then Newfoundland and Labrador.
First Nations are geographically located in the southern region of Canada, in the provinces bordering the United States. Ontario, Quebec and British Columbia are the most densely populated provinces with First Nations individuals.
Metis Indigenous communities are geographically located in the southern region of Canada, in the provinces bordering the United States. Ontario, Alberta, Manitoba, and British Columbia are where the largest populations of Metis Indigenous peoples live in Canada.

Females tend to have higher mental health and food insecurity scores than do males on average. Among indigenous populations, First Nations off reserve tend to have the highest rates of poor mental health and food insecurity scores. Nonindigenous population has the lowest rates of food insecurity and poor mental health.
Indigenous populations are more likely to face food insecurity than their nonindigenous counterparts. Inuit in Nunavet, Canada have the highest rates of food insecurity, with 61.85% being food insecure. There is not a significant difference in food insecurity between nonindigenous male and female populations. However, indigenous females are more likely to be food insecure than male indigenous.
